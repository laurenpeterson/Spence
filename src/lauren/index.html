about

I'm Lauren Spence and I'm a front-end web developer.

In 2013, I graduated from Cal Poly San Luis Obispo with a degree in Graphic Communication and a minor in English. Since then I've worked at a local Marketing Agency as a front-end developer. "Agency Life" means my work has been entirely project-based, and the type of projects I've gotten to work on span the gamut. I've contributed to cutting-edge web apps and sites, and I've made my copy-paste fingers sore entering content for the agency's custom-built app. I've built many a page from scratch, using CSS pre-processors, templating languages, and build tools, as well as worked with myriad content management systems, including AEM, Wordpress, and Expression Engine.

Bridal Shower Invitations

Tools: Watercolor, Photoshop, InDesign

I created these invitations for a bridal shower that my friends and I hosted. I used watercolor to paint the hearts, then scanned the finished piece and touched it up in Photoshop, adding in some hearts in Photoshop where they were more sparse. I also used Photoshop to manipulate the hearts into an umbrella shape to play with the bridal "showers" pun. I then used this as a background and did the type in InDesign. These are two concept designs I did - the bride chose the one on the left.

Hand Made Swing Dress

Tools: Rayon, Pfaff Ambition Essential Sewing Machine

I made this simple swing dress myself, and drafted my own pattern using an existing garment I liked. For the construction, I used French seams and two different methods of bias binding for the neck and arm holes. This was my first time working with rayon, and I'm very proud of the results.

Hand Made Quilts

Tools: Pfaff Ambition Essential Sewing Machine, quilting cottons, quilter's thread

Since I discovered this practical art form, I've made more than 10 quilts, ranging in size from small wall hangings to baby quilts, to full-sized bed quilts. I've followed the patterns of other talented sewists (center quilt here), drafted my own pattern based off an inspiration photo (here), and figured out my own designs based on foundational quilter's blocks.

Wedding Invitation Suite

Tools: InDesign, Photoshop, Letterpress, Linocut Stamp

I designed and created the many pieces of stationery for my wedding. I started at Cal Poly's Shakespeare Press Museum, where I typeset and printed the save-the-date cards by hand using an antique letterpress press. I printed the invitations and RSVP cards digitally, then used a Linocut stamp I carved myself to add the embellishment. The RSVP postcards have a painting by my aunt, Karin Shelton on the back. I cut out envelope liners from various handmade papers, and hand-lettered the address on each envelope. I used a custom-ordered stamp for the return address.
